# Massachusetts Building Codes and Requirements

**State:** Massachusetts (MA)
**Building Code:** 780 CMR (Massachusetts State Building Code)
**Base Code:** 2021 International Building Code with MA amendments
**Energy Code:** 2021 IECC with MA amendments
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024 (current edition)

---

## Overview

Massachusetts has a statewide building code that supersedes local codes. While municipalities can request amendments, the core code is consistent across the state. Massachusetts is known for having one of the more stringent building codes in the nation, particularly for energy efficiency.

All work requiring a permit must be performed by or supervised by appropriately licensed contractors. Homeowner exemptions are limited.

---

## Permit Requirements

### Work That REQUIRES a Permit

**Structural Work:**
- Any structural modifications (walls, beams, posts)
- Room additions
- Decks and porches
- Basement finishing
- Garage construction or conversion
- Roof structural changes

**Electrical Work:**
- New circuits or panel upgrades
- Adding or relocating outlets/switches
- Installing hardwired fixtures
- Generator hookups
- EV charger installation
- Any work beyond simple device replacement

**Plumbing Work:**
- New fixtures (toilet, sink, shower)
- Relocating plumbing
- Water heater replacement
- Water main work
- Drain/sewer line work
- Gas line work (requires plumber with gas ticket)

**HVAC Work:**
- Furnace or boiler replacement
- AC system installation
- Ductwork modifications
- Mini-split installation
- Gas appliance installation or replacement

**Roofing:**
- Full roof replacement
- Structural roof repairs
- Adding skylights

**Other:**
- Window or door replacement (if changing size/location)
- Siding replacement (full replacement requires permit in most towns)
- Swimming pools and hot tubs
- Fences over 6 feet
- Sheds over 200 square feet
- Demolition

### Work That Typically Does NOT Require a Permit

- Painting and wallpapering
- Flooring replacement (cosmetic)
- Cabinet installation (no plumbing changes)
- Replacing fixtures with same type (same location)
- Minor repairs and maintenance
- Window replacement (same size, same location)
- Gutter installation
- Landscaping

**Note:** Always verify with your local building department. Some towns have stricter requirements.

## Contractor Licensing Requirements

Massachusetts requires state licenses for most trade work. **It is illegal for unlicensed individuals to perform licensed work, even for homeowners on their own property** (with limited exceptions).

### Construction Supervisor License (CSL)

**Required for:** Any construction, reconstruction, alteration, or repair work

| License Type | Requirements | Scope |
|-------------|--------------|-------|
| Unrestricted CSL | Exam + experience | All residential and commercial |
| Restricted CSL | Exam + experience | One- and two-family dwellings only |
| Specialty CSL | Exam + experience | Specific trade (siding, roofing, etc.) |

**Verify License:** https://www.mass.gov/how-to/check-a-license

**Homeowner Exemption:** Homeowners may act as their own contractor for their primary residence IF:
- They obtain proper permits
- They use licensed subcontractors for electrical, plumbing, and gas work
- They do not sell the property within 12 months

### Electrical Licenses

| License Type | Requirements | Scope |
|-------------|--------------|-------|
| Master Electrician | Exam + 4 years as journeyman | Can pull permits, supervise |
| Journeyman Electrician | Exam + 8,000 hours apprenticeship | Work under Master |
| Apprentice | Enrolled in program | Work under supervision only |

**Critical:** Only Master Electricians can pull electrical permits. Homeowners cannot do their own electrical work requiring permits.

**Verify:** Board of State Examiners of Electricians

### Plumbing and Gas Licenses

| License Type | Requirements | Scope |
|-------------|--------------|-------|
| Master Plumber | Exam + 6 years experience | Full plumbing, can pull permits |
| Journeyman Plumber | Exam + 5 years experience | Work under Master |
| Apprentice | Registered | Work under supervision |

**Gas Fitting:** Plumbers with gas fitting certification can work on gas lines. Separate license required.

**Critical:** Homeowners cannot do their own plumbing or gas work that requires permits.

**Verify:** Board of State Examiners of Plumbers and Gas Fitters

### HVAC Licenses

| License Type | Requirements | Scope |
|-------------|--------------|-------|
| Oil Burner Technician | Exam | Oil heating equipment |
| Refrigeration Technician | Exam + EPA 608 | AC and heat pump systems |
| Sheet Metal License | For ductwork over certain thresholds |

### Home Improvement Contractor Registration (HIC)

**Required for:** Any contractor doing home improvement work over $100

- All contractors must register with the state
- Provides consumer protection
- Registration number must appear on contracts

**Verify:** https://www.mass.gov/how-to/check-contractor-registration

## Key Building Code Requirements

### Energy Code (IECC 2021 with MA Amendments)

**Insulation Requirements (Zone 5A - South Shore):**

| Component | Minimum R-Value |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Attic (ceiling) | R-49 |
| Walls (cavity) | R-20 or R-13 + R-5 continuous |
| Floors over unconditioned space | R-30 |
| Basement walls | R-15 continuous or R-19 cavity |
| Slab edge | R-10, 2 ft depth |

**Window Requirements:**
- Maximum U-factor: 0.30
- Maximum SHGC: 0.40 (or no requirement in Zone 5)

**Air Sealing:**
- Blower door test required for new construction
- Maximum 3 ACH50 (air changes per hour at 50 pascals)

**Stretch Code:** Many MA municipalities have adopted the "Stretch Code" which adds additional energy requirements above base code. Check with your town.

**Specialized Stretch Code:** Newer, even more stringent code available for towns that adopt it.

### Roofing Requirements

**Ice and Water Shield:**
- REQUIRED: Minimum 24 inches from eave edge
- Applies in all areas where average January temperature is 25F or less (all of MA)
- Recommended: Extend 3-6 feet in ice dam-prone applications

**Roof Ventilation:**
- Minimum 1 sq ft ventilation per 150 sq ft attic floor area
- Can be reduced to 1:300 with proper vapor barrier or balanced intake/exhaust

**Snow Load:**
- Ground snow load: 35-70 psf depending on location
- Design roof load: Approximately 25-50 psf
- South Shore typically uses 40 psf ground load

### Electrical Requirements

**GFCI Protection Required:**
- Bathrooms (all outlets)
- Kitchens (countertop outlets within 6 feet of sink)
- Garages
- Outdoors
- Basements (unfinished)
- Crawl spaces
- Laundry areas (within 6 feet of sink)
- Near pools and hot tubs

**AFCI Protection Required:**
- Bedrooms
- Living rooms
- Hallways
- Closets
- Other habitable rooms

**Smoke Detectors:**
- Hardwired with battery backup
- Interconnected
- Required in every bedroom, outside sleeping areas, every level
- Photoelectric type required near kitchens and bathrooms

**Carbon Monoxide Detectors:**
- Required on every level with sleeping areas
- Required adjacent to fossil fuel appliances

**Panel Location:**
- Must be readily accessible
- Not in bathrooms or over stairs
- Working clearance: 30" wide x 36" deep x 78" high

### Plumbing Requirements

**Water Heater:**
- Temperature/pressure relief valve required
- Discharge pipe to within 6 inches of floor or exterior
- Expansion tank required on closed systems
- Seismic strapping not required in MA (but good practice)

**Fixtures:**
- Anti-scald valves required on showers and tubs
- Low-flow fixtures per water conservation code

**Venting:**
- All fixtures must be properly vented
- Air admittance valves (AAVs) allowed with restrictions

### Egress Requirements

**Bedroom Windows:**
- Minimum opening: 5.7 sq ft (5.0 sq ft at grade)
- Minimum width: 20 inches
- Minimum height: 24 inches
- Maximum sill height: 44 inches from floor

**Basement Egress:**
- If bedroom in basement, must have compliant egress window
- Window well required if below grade
- Ladder or steps in window well over 44 inches deep

### Fire Safety

**Smoke Barriers:**
- Between attached garage and dwelling
- 1/2" drywall minimum on garage side
- Self-closing door required

**Sprinklers:**
- Not required for one- and two-family dwellings in MA
- May be required in some condominium and multi-family situations

## Septic System Requirements (Title 5)

Massachusetts has comprehensive septic regulations under 310 CMR 15 (Title 5).

### Inspection Requirements

**Title 5 Inspection Required:**
- Within 2 years before property sale
- Before building permit for additions affecting flow
- Within 3 years before refinancing (some lenders)
- When ordered by Board of Health

**System Pumping:**
- Required before Title 5 inspection
- Recommended every 2-3 years for typical household

### System Requirements

**Setbacks:**
- 10 feet from foundation
- 50-100 feet from private well (depending on design flow)
- 100 feet from surface water
- 10 feet from property lines

**Design Flow:**
- Based on number of bedrooms
- 110 gallons per bedroom per day (minimum)
- Additions that add bedrooms require septic review

**Failure Criteria:**
- Sewage backup
- Breakout to surface
- Contamination of nearby water
- Structural failure of components

### Repair and Replacement

**When System Fails:**
- Board of Health notification required
- Repair or replacement within specified timeframe
- Upgrade to current code may be required

**Innovative/Alternative Systems:**
- Allowed with Board of Health approval
- Required when conventional system not feasible
- More complex maintenance requirements

## Historic District Considerations

Many South Shore communities have local historic districts or National Register historic districts.

### Common Restrictions

**Exterior Changes:**
- Window replacement style and material
- Siding material and profile
- Roof material
- Addition design and placement
- Fence and outbuilding design

**Review Process:**
- Historic District Commission approval required
- Public hearing may be required
- Can add weeks/months to project timeline

### Towns with Historic Districts

- Hingham (significant historic district downtown)
- Cohasset (village center)
- Duxbury (multiple districts)
- Scituate (harbor area)
- Marshfield (limited areas)

**Recommendation:** Always check with town building department about historic district status before planning exterior work.

## Permit Process

### Typical Steps

1. **Consult Building Department** - Discuss project and requirements
2. **Submit Application** - Include plans, specifications, contractor info
3. **Plan Review** - Building department reviews for code compliance
4. **Permit Issued** - Once approved, work can begin
5. **Inspections** - Required at various stages
6. **Final Inspection** - Upon completion
7. **Certificate of Occupancy** - For additions or new construction

### Inspection Stages

**Typical Inspection Points:**
- Foundation (before backfill)
- Framing (before covering)
- Rough electrical, plumbing, HVAC
- Insulation (before drywall)
- Final

### Timeline

| Project Type | Typical Review Time |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Simple permit (water heater, etc.) | 1-3 days |
| Moderate project (bathroom remodel) | 1-2 weeks |
| Addition or major renovation | 2-6 weeks |
| New construction | 4-12 weeks |

## Penalties for Unpermitted Work

**Consequences:**
- Stop work order
- Fines (vary by municipality, typically $100-500+ per day)
- Required removal of work
- Difficulty selling home (title issues)
- Insurance claim complications
- Liability if injury occurs

**Discovery:**
- Building department inspections
- Neighbor complaints
- Sale inspection
- Insurance claims
- Future permit applications

**Remediation:**
- Apply for permit retroactively (if work is to code)
- Open walls for inspection
- Modify or remove non-compliant work
- Pay fines and fees
